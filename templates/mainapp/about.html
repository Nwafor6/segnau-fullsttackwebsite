Who are we??

The Nnamdi Azikiwe University Geophysical Society is a geoscience community that is passionate and committed the bridging the gap between the geoscience industry and the academia.

Brief history

The Nnamdi Azikiwe University Geophysical Society was re-established in 2016 by Mrs Vivian-Oguadinma Dike (Ph.D). Since its re-establishment, the chapter has grown from a pioneer membership of ten (10) student members to over two hundred (200) students and faculty members.

The chapter has been involved several activities that are aimed at creating awareness of the geosciences as well as equipping and training her members on the necessary skills for a geoscientist in this digital age.

Some of these activities include field camps/field trips, physical and virtual excursions, nano conference, presentation series, outreach programs, research clusters as well as capacity-building workshops and trainings.

We also engage our members in socio-academic competitions to foster team work among them, thus preparing them for the broader demands of the geoscience workspace.

As a chapter, one of our core values is the development of technical, research and intellectual competences of our chapter members. All these are made possible through the unending support of the SEG Foundation, highly esteemed faculty members and the chapter alumni.

The chapter’s achievements include
  • Best Student Chapter (2022).
  • Best Student Chapter (2022)
  • Summit Level Student Chapter (2021 and 2022)
  • SEG Scholarship Awards (2016, 2020, 2021 and 2022)
  • SEG Student Chapter Outreach Grant Awards (2018, 2020 and 2021)
  • SEG Field Camp Grant Awards (2020, 2021 and 2022)
  • SEG/Chevron Students Leadership Symposium Travel Grant Awards (2018, 2021 and 2022)
  • SEG Geoscientists without Borders Grant Award (2021)
